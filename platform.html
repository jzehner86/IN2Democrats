Our 2026 Platform

After 20 years of Republican rule, Hoosiers are paying more for housing, utilities, and healthcare while wages stagnate. Indiana Democrats are putting forward common-sense solutions that put families first.

Affordability for Every Hoosier

Hoosiers have been crushed under rising costs — from healthcare to rent to utilities. Every policy we champion is rooted in the real stories of families doing everything right but still falling behind.

Reduce Healthcare Costs

Access to healthcare is a human right, not a luxury. Republicans shut down the federal government to deny 364,000 Hoosiers access to the ACA, doubling their insurance premiums — while insurance company profits hit record highs. Indiana Democrats will work for patients, not big insurance.

  • Fully restore health insurance subsidies to lower costs for Hoosier patients
  • Extend prescription price negotiations beyond just Medicaid
  • Require insurance companies and pharmacies to pass any savings from prescription rebates or hospital cost reductions back to the consumer
  • Protect Hoosiers burdened with medical debt — prohibit liens on primary residences, require hospitals to offer payment plans, and cap monthly payments for low-income patients
Lower Utility Bills

Indiana ranks 3rd in the nation for utility shutoffs. A Republican majority has stood by while bills skyrocket past what families can afford. Democrats are pushing legislation to keep utility companies beholden to Hoosiers — not Wall Street.

  • Eliminate the sales tax on utilities
  • Enact a moratorium on utility rate hikes
  • End tax breaks for data centers and require them to pay for their own energy costs
Improve Access to Housing

Indiana leads the nation in evictions. Property taxes and rent keep climbing while housing stock stays limited. The Republican supermajority's big "fix" saves the average household just over $200 a year — not enough to cover a car payment. Democrats offer meaningful solutions to keep Hoosiers in their homes, affordably.

  • Create a path to first-time home ownership with buyer tax breaks
  • Increase the renters' tax deduction and crack down on negligent landlords by allowing tenants to withhold rent until essential repairs are made
  • Allow faith institutions to build affordable housing units on their property and reduce restrictions on tiny homes on personal property

Education & an Economy that Works for Workers

A strong economy is built by workers, not CEOs. That means investing in the building blocks that allow families to thrive — childcare, public schools, fair wages, and the freedom to take time for your family.

Treat Childcare as Essential

Indiana only has childcare spots for 41% of the children who need them. As a result, nearly 60% of Hoosier parents have cut work hours or left jobs entirely. Without childcare, parents can't work, businesses suffer, and brain drain accelerates. Democrats are standing up for parents and families.

  • Fully fund the Child Care Development Fund so low-income families can afford to get to work
  • Implement universal pre-K for 3 and 4 year olds
  • Improve childcare worker pay and remove employment barriers, such as the ban on workers having their own children in the room
Fully Fund Public Schools & Better Pay for Teachers

Indiana's public schools educate 90% of students — yet Republicans have stripped over $500 million from them to fund voucher expansions. Book bans and speech regulations violate 1st Amendment protections and make teachers targets. Democrats remain committed to fully supporting public education and the teachers who make it work.

  • Raise starting teacher salaries to $60,000 to slow the loss of teachers to other states and careers
  • Fully fund public schools
  • Require all schools above a free and reduced lunch threshold to apply for corporation-wide free meals — ensuring every child eats while reducing administrative costs
Implement Paid Family Leave

Paid family leave lets workers care for a new child or aging parent without sacrificing their paycheck. It helps businesses retain skilled workers, improves maternal and infant health outcomes, and is supported by over 75% of Americans. Most Hoosiers don't have it. Republicans have refused to act. Democrats will.

  • Incentivize employers to create paid family leave programs
  • Create a statewide family leave program funded through very small payroll tax contributions
Maintain High-Paying Jobs IN Indiana

At $7.25 an hour — the lowest permitted by federal law — a Hoosier must work three full-time jobs to afford a two-bedroom apartment. Indiana's stagnant wages are a direct contributor to the highest foreclosure rate in the nation. Democrats understand that a strong economy is built by workers, not CEOs.

  • Raise Indiana's minimum wage to a livable amount so full-time workers can afford the basics
  • Invest in community improvement, strong public schools, public health, and parks — the things businesses need to recruit workers
  • Continue to fight to ensure U.S. companies stay U.S. companies
  • Champion unions to ensure workers earn high pay and benefits reflective of their hard work
  • Demand that economic development initiatives, including data centers, pay for their own utilities, invest in neighborhoods, and ensure local residents fill available jobs

Freedom

You are not free when the government regulates who you love, tells you what to do with your own body, or denies you opportunities because of the color of your skin. Democrats stand for individual freedoms — for every Hoosier.

Support Veterans

Hoosier heroes deserve to be honored with real support, not empty rhetoric. Indiana must be a state that prioritizes every veteran's wellbeing — including fighting back when the Republican supermajority cuts their access to benefits.

  • Fight Republican cuts to veterans' access to the Military Family Relief Fund
  • Require every county to hire a County Service Officer to ensure veterans statewide can access services, and establish a fund to cover their salaries
  • Require discharge counseling for returning veterans to evaluate needed services and ensure they are available
Legalize Marijuana

84% of Hoosiers believe marijuana should be legal in some form. No one in Indiana should go to jail for something that is legal in 40 states and D.C. Our neighboring states are capturing millions of Indiana tax dollars. After more than a decade of Republicans ignoring Democrat proposals, it is time to bring Indiana into the modern era.

  • Fully legalize marijuana and create the regulatory framework to govern its sale and use
  • Expunge records for those with marijuana convictions
Restore Abortion Rights

59% of Hoosiers support abortion access in all or some cases, yet the Republican supermajority forced through an extreme abortion ban. Since then, 16 hospital obstetrics departments have closed, creating women's healthcare deserts in 25% of Indiana's 92 counties. Indiana now has the 3rd highest maternal mortality rate in the nation. This is a crisis — and Democrats will act.

  • Restore abortion rights to Roe v. Wade status
  • Restore funding to Planned Parenthood for wellness exams, STI testing and treatment, and birth control
  • Return privacy and dignity to women's healthcare decisions
Live Safely in Our Communities

Gun violence is now the leading cause of death for children. Police positions go unfilled. Mass shootings target public gatherings. Republicans have poured $170 billion into ICE — more than the combined budgets of all local and state law enforcement agencies — while doing nothing to make communities safer. Indiana Democrats protect the 2nd Amendment and protect lives.

  • Incentivize safe gun storage in homes to reduce youth access to firearms
  • Support the Indiana Gun Crimes Task Force — a multi-agency partnership focused on seizing illegal firearms and targeting violent offenders
  • Require schools to provide mental health services as a condition of receiving secured school funds
  • Raise the age to purchase a firearm to 21 and ban the sale of automatic weapons
Freedom from Discrimination

Government should represent all of us — not regulate who we love, fund one religion above others, or deny opportunity based on race or identity. Republicans have banned voluntary DEI programs, refuse to remove Indiana's unconstitutional same-sex marriage ban, and fund faith-based initiatives for a single religion. Democrats have always stood for individual freedoms.

  • Either stop tax-funding of faith-based initiatives, or make the funding equally available to all religions
  • Remove the ban on same-sex marriage still written in Indiana's code
  • Restore DEI programs so every Hoosier has the opportunity to succeed

There Is a Path Forward

Indiana Democrats are aligned with the majority of Hoosiers who want a fair shot and a little breathing room. Help us build the Indiana every family deserves.
